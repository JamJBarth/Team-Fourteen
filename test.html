RULES & POLICIES
Basic Unit Policy
High Commanding Officers (Warrant Officer+)

As a soldier of the San Andreas Army, whenever you wish to approach any High Commanding Staff, you will have to properly salute the Commanding Officer before you state your needs and requirements. Additionally, whenever a High Command Officer arrives in a location, you MUST relay the proper Military salute. The default salute for the SANG is ((/ghands 10.))

Reassembly Location & Policy

During your service time in San Andreas Army, you will hear the word 'Reassemble', which basically means: All soldiers MUST return to base (10-1), replenish their HP & AR, then lineup in front of the Memorial Site (outside by the lockers), and await further commands. The soldiers will line in a proper, column formation and await the High Commanding Officer to arrive on-scene. The formation will be as shown:

Column Formation:-

Mostly used during the platoon's reassembly.

File Formation:-

In narrow areas, the Commanding Officer might order you to form a Single File Formation, which allows the troops to fit into that narrow place while still maintaining their security measures.

Line Formation:-

In Open-Areas, the Commanding Officer might order you to form a Line Formation, which covers almost the entire open-area. This formation can also be used in open-area turfs.

Base Access Policy

Since this Fort is not equipped with a Visitor's Center, all non-military personnel that approach and/or request access to the installation is to be denied. The following scenarios are the only ones admissible for entry: - Person(s) that were instructed to access the installation for an event, meeting, or visitation by a High Command (W.O [R5] and beyond) - Civilian Law Enforcement Officials (LEOs) that require access for a matter that involves the Army - Fire & Medical Officials that require access to aid and/or provide sufficient support for troops, civilian(s) (if present), or LEOs that require medical attention. - Department of Defense (DoD) Officials

Any person(s), military or not, that approach the Front Entrance of Fort Randolph must follow the following requirements to access the installation:
Civilian(s) access to the installation:

- Personal Identification Card
- State Driver's License (If applicable)
- State's Vehicle Registration
- Proof of Insurance All forms of ID used for entry must contain a valid expiration date, photo must appear to match the individual presenting it and show no signs of mutilation or alteration.

Law Enforcement Officials access to the installation:

Fire & Medical Officials access to the installation:

- Valid form of government (local, state or federal) issued personal identification card. ((/showbadge)) & ((/showid))

Base Defense Strategy

Base defense should be governed by considering the factors of mission, enemy, terrain, weather, troops, support and time available (including all friendly forces). Additionally, careful consideration should be given to the protection of any key strategic or operational assets located in the base. The current base commander (Highest H.R available) must evaluate the current threat and determine its level.

Level I Threats

Includes any indirect, unharmful threat (No firearms suspected or holstered firearms) to the Fort's surroundings, Area of operation, and/or troops within. Any breaches through any of the Fort's entrances and/or grounds will be treated as follows: Class A Act: A Class-A Act includes a First-Degree warning to be transmitted to the intruder(s), giving them sufficient time to leave the Fort's premises (with an escort). At this stage, troops should have their weapons out with the safety pin [ON]. Class B Act: A Class-B Act includes a Second-Degree warning to be transmitted to the intruder(s). At this stage, troops should fire a warning shot nearby, with additional units en-routing to the scene of breach. At this level, no vehicles of any sort should be operated, apart from the MP's Gate Patriot.

Level II Threats

Includes any direct (Holstered Firearms suspected) threats to the Fort's surroundings, Area of operation and/or troops within. Any breaches through any of the Fort's entrances and/or grounds will be treated as follows: Class A Act: A Class-A Act includes a First-Degree warning to be transmitted to the intruder(s). At this stage, troops should fire a warning shot nearby, with additional units en-routing to the scene of breach. The intruder(s) must carefully be extracted from the scene to the exterior of the base. Class B Act: A Class-B Act includes the authorization of 'Shoot-to-Kill' policy. At this stage, troops are allowed to fire injuring and/or lethal shots to the intruder(s), depending on the severity of the threat at hand. Troops are advised to minimize the usage of lethal power as much as possible, to avoid combat and life losses. At this level, troops should operate the following vehicles:- - A Hunter should patrol the AO for any further breaches - An X amount of Patriot(s) should patrol the interior and exterior of the Fort for any further breaches - An X amount of troop(s) should operate and function the Guard Towers with direct firing power. - An X amount of troop(s) should approach and handle the breach at-hand.

Level III Threats

Includes any direct threats to the Fort's surroundings, Area of operation, and/or troops within. Any breaches through any of the Fort's entrances and/or grounds will be treated as follows: Class A Act: A Class-A Act includes full authorization of 'Shoot-to-Kill' policy. At this stage, troops have the full authorization to use lethal rounds at any intruder(s) within the Fort's grounds. At this level, troops should quickly operate the following vehicles:- - An X amount of Hunter(s) should patrol the AO for any further breaches - An X amount of Patriot(s) should patrol the interior and exterior of the Fort for any further breaches - An X amount of troop(s) should operate and function the Guard Towers with direct firing power. Depending on the severity of the threat, tanks may or may not be used. This decision is left for the base commander to decide based on the threat's level.

If any person(s) fails to abide by the instructions given to them by the Military Police Personnel, then troops have the right to open fire and shoot-to-kill, under Section 22-40503.01 of L.S law, which allows Military Troops to use lethal force against any civilian, LEO and/or emergency unit that failed to follow instructions within Military grounds.
City Entrance Policy

The Posse Comitatus Act 18, Section & Clause 1385 of the Constitution.

The Posse Comitatus Act outlaws the willful use of any part of the Army or Air Force to execute the law unless expressly authorized by the Constitution or an act of Congress.

The Martial Law, Article I, Section 8, Clause 15 of the Constitution.

Martial law is the imposition of direct military control of normal civil functions or suspension of civil law by a government, especially in response to a temporary emergency where civil forces are overwhelmed, or in occupied territory.



With the current Acts active and implemented, we, as the San Andreas National Guards have the full authority to access the city of Los Santos and operate with full Law Enforcement powers to execute the law as authorized by the Constitution. However, as Congress has the upper-saying of this act, we are limited on what we can operate within the city's grounds.

In terms of vehicle(s):-

The San Andreas National Guard is only allowed to operate the following vehicles within the city of Los Santos grounds (Unless the severity requires further usage of Heavy Vehicles): - An X amount of Marked Cruiser(s)
- An X amount of Marked Military-Grade Ambulance(s)
- An X amount of Marked Patriot(s)
- An X amount of Marked Enforcer(s)
- An X amount of Marked Flatbed(s)

The following vehicles are prohibited from entry to the city:
- Military Tanks (Rhino)
- Military Apache (Hunter)
- Military F16 Jets (Hydra)

The mentioned vehicles may only enter IF requested by the Governor of Los Santos, in-case of War, or the agreement of all Local, State, and federal agencies.
In terms of equipment & weaponry:-

The San Andreas National Guard is allowed to use all necessary weapons and equipment within the city of Los Santos apart from the following:
- Military Grade RPGs
- Military Drones
- Military Grenades
